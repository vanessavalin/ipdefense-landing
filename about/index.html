What we actually do

Brand abuse on app platforms follows predictable patterns. Someone registers a similar app name, copies your icon or screenshots, stuffs your brand keywords into their metadata, and starts diverting the organic search traffic that your marketing spend and product quality earned. On social platforms, impersonators create accounts under your brand name and intercept your community.

Platforms will act on well-documented complaints — but the complaint process requires knowing which violation category applies, what evidence each platform needs, how to write a complaint that gets routed to the right enforcement team, and how to escalate when first-level complaints don't result in action.

That operational knowledge is what IP Defense provides. We have run this process across Apple, Google, Meta, TikTok, X, YouTube, and web registrars. We know what works, what gets complaints rejected, and how to escalate when the standard process stalls.

Why brand enforcement needs a specialist

The most common failure mode we see is a founder or growth manager spending hours filing complaint reports through platform web forms, receiving no response, and concluding that "the platform won't do anything." In most of those cases, the complaint was filed under the wrong violation category, lacked the specific evidence the platform's enforcement team needed, or was submitted to a first-level triage queue that doesn't result in action for contextual violations.

Platform enforcement is a process. Done correctly, with the right evidence and the right escalation path, the majority of clear violations get resolved. Our 200+ resolved cases across six platforms is the proof point.
