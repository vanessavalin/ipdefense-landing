How to Report an Infringing App on the App Store and Google Play

By IP Defense · App Brand Protection · 10 min read

Filing a complaint about a copycat or infringing app sounds straightforward. In practice, most first-time complaints result in no action — not because the violation isn't real, but because the complaint is missing the specific documentation that platform enforcement teams need to act.

This guide walks through the Apple and Google complaint processes in detail: what each form requires, what evidence to gather first, what mistakes to avoid, and what to do when the initial complaint doesn't get results.

Before you file: gather your evidence

Both Apple and Google make enforcement decisions based on the documentation you provide. They receive high volumes of complaints and cannot independently investigate each one. The quality of your evidence package is the single biggest factor in whether your complaint results in action.

Before submitting anything, assemble the following:

  • Screenshots of the infringing app's listing — full page, including the developer name, app name, icon, description, and screenshots section
  • Screenshots of your app's listing — for direct comparison
  • Screenshots showing both apps in search results — demonstrate the confusion in context
  • Your app's release date and original publication — establish that your app predates the infringing one
  • Trademark documentation if applicable — a registered trademark significantly strengthens a name or logo complaint
  • Specific policy citations — identify which App Store or Google Play policies the infringing app violates
  • Direct links — the exact App Store or Google Play URL of the infringing app

Timestamp everything. Screenshot your device's status bar (showing date and time) in each capture, or use a tool that embeds metadata. Platforms occasionally respond weeks after your initial complaint, and having dated evidence matters if the infringing developer tries to claim priority.

Reporting to Apple (App Store)

Apple IP Infringement Form

https://reportinfringement.apple.com

Also available: App Store listing → scroll to bottom → "Report a Problem" (for end users, less formal)

Selecting the right violation type

Apple's form asks you to categorize the infringement type. Choosing the wrong category results in the complaint being routed to a team that can't act on it. The most relevant categories for brand abuse:

  • Trademark infringement — use this when the app name or logo replicates or closely imitates your registered trademark
  • App Store guideline violation — use this for metadata violations, keyword stuffing of your brand name, or misleading descriptions
  • Copyright infringement — use this when screenshots, artwork, or written content has been directly copied
  • Impersonation — use this when the app is presenting itself as your company or product without authorization

If multiple violation types apply, file separate complaints for each. This routes each complaint to the appropriate enforcement team rather than consolidating them in a single queue.

What to write in the description

Apple's complaint form includes a free-text description field. Most unsuccessful complaints write something like: "This app is copying my app." Effective descriptions include:

  • Your app name, App Store URL, and original release date
  • The infringing app's name and App Store URL
  • Specific elements being copied (name similarity, icon similarity, screenshot duplication)
  • Which specific App Store guideline or policy is being violated (cite the guideline number if possible)
  • The harm being caused (user confusion, diverted installs, brand damage)
  • Any prior communication with the infringing developer (if applicable)
  1. Gather all evidence before opening the form — the form has a session timeout
  2. Go to reportinfringement.apple.com and select your complaint category
  3. Enter your contact information — Apple will follow up via email
  4. Provide your app's information and the infringing app's App Store URL
  5. Write a specific, factual description — avoid emotional language, stick to facts
  6. Attach your screenshots (Apple accepts PNG and JPG attachments)
  7. Submit and note your case reference number

Apple response timeline

Apple typically acknowledges complaints within 1–3 business days. Investigation and action can take 3–10 business days for clear-cut cases. More complex cases, or those where Apple requests additional information, can take 2–4 weeks. If the infringing app is removed, you typically receive an email confirmation, though Apple doesn't always communicate the resolution.

Reporting to Google (Google Play)

Google Play IP complaint form

https://support.google.com/googleplay/android-developer/contact/takedown

Also available: On any Play Store listing → three-dot menu → "Flag as inappropriate" (for basic reports)

Key differences from Apple's process

Google Play's enforcement process differs from Apple's in several important ways:

  • Automated initial review: Google uses automated systems to triage complaints before human review. Clear policy violations with well-documented evidence are more likely to be actioned quickly by the automated layer.
  • Developer response opportunity: Google typically notifies the accused developer and gives them an opportunity to respond before removing the app. This can extend timelines.
  • Account-level enforcement: If Google determines a developer account has engaged in repeated policy violations, it can suspend the entire account rather than removing individual apps. This is more powerful than single-app removal but takes longer.
  • Trademark-specific form: Google has a separate trademark complaint form specifically for brand name and logo violations, distinct from general policy complaints. Using the specific form gets faster routing.

Google Play trademark complaint: If your issue is specifically a name or logo infringement and you have a registered trademark, use Google's trademark-specific complaint path. It routes to a different team than general policy complaints and has historically resulted in faster resolution.

Timeline expectations for Google Play

Google Play initial response: typically 5–7 business days. Resolution for straightforward violations: 10–20 business days. Complex cases or developer appeals can extend this to 6–8 weeks. Unlike Apple, Google is more likely to ask for additional documentation after your initial submission.

Social platform reporting

If impersonation extends to social media, each platform has its own reporting mechanism:

  • Instagram/Facebook: Report via the profile → three-dot menu → "Report" → "Pretending to be something else" → "A business or organization"
  • TikTok: Profile page → three-dot menu → "Report" → "Impersonation" → "Impersonating a business or brand"
  • X (Twitter): Profile → three-dot menu → "Report" → "They're pretending to be me or someone else" → "A company, brand, or organization"
  • YouTube: Report channel via the About tab → Flag icon → "Spam or misleading" → "Impersonation"

Social platform impersonation complaints are typically faster than app store complaints (24–72 hours for clear cases) but the evidence threshold is also different. Focus the complaint on the impersonation of your brand identity rather than on content claims.

When the complaint doesn't get results

First-level complaints result in no action in a significant percentage of cases — particularly for violations that require contextual judgment (name similarity, visual similarity) rather than clear-cut policy breaches. When this happens:

  • Resubmit with more specific evidence — review your original complaint for specificity gaps and resubmit with additional documentation
  • Escalate through the developer support channel — both Apple and Google have developer relations teams that can be contacted separately from the public enforcement forms
  • File multiple complaint categories simultaneously — if you initially filed under trademark, file a separate complaint under App Store guidelines; different teams handle each
  • Contact the infringing developer directly — in some cases a direct cease-and-desist communication resolves the issue without platform involvement. This requires careful handling to avoid complicating the enforcement case.

Document every interaction. Save every case reference number, every email response, every acknowledgment. If escalation becomes necessary, the history of prior complaints is evidence that platform-level enforcement was attempted.

Frequently asked questions

Do I need a registered trademark to file a complaint?
No. Both Apple and Google accept brand complaints from companies without registered trademarks, under their general guidelines against apps that create user confusion. However, a registered trademark significantly strengthens the complaint and typically results in faster resolution. Common law trademark rights (established through commercial use) can also support a complaint, though they're harder to document.
Can I report an app that uses a similar name but isn't identical?
Yes. Platform policies prohibit apps that create a "likelihood of confusion" with established brands, which explicitly includes similar-but-not-identical names. In your complaint, document the similarity and explain specifically how a user could mistake the app for yours. Examples of user reviews on the copycat's listing expressing confusion are particularly compelling evidence.
What happens to the infringing app's reviews and ranking after removal?
When an app is removed by the platform, its listing is taken down entirely, including its reviews and rankings. Those reviews do not transfer to your app. Your organic search results will typically improve within one to three store index cycles as the competing listing is no longer present.
Can the developer re-publish the same app after removal?
Yes, though Apple and Google track violations. A developer whose app was removed for policy violations faces additional scrutiny on re-submission. Repeated violations can result in developer account suspension, which prevents re-publishing. Tracking whether the app re-appears after removal is part of ongoing brand monitoring.
How long does enforcement take from start to finish?
For a well-documented complaint with clear evidence, straightforward violations typically resolve in 2–4 weeks across both platforms. Complex cases, developer appeals, or multi-platform enforcement can take 6–12 weeks. Having complete evidence from the start — rather than gathering it after submission when platforms ask for it — is the single biggest variable in resolution time.
